TIGER

Tigers are the largest wild cats in the world, known for their striking orange coat with black stripes. They are solitary hunters and primarily prey on large ungulates.

Tigers are powerful and agile predators, capable of running at high speeds and swimming well. They are known for their strength and stealth, making them effective hunters in their natural habitats. Tigers are solitary animals, preferring to live alone except during mating season or when raising cubs. They are territorial and mark their territory with scent markings and scratch marks on trees.

Tigers are found in a variety of habitats, including tropical forests, grasslands, and mangrove swamps. They play a crucial role in maintaining the balance of their ecosystems by controlling prey populations. Unfortunately, tigers face numerous threats, including habitat loss, poaching, and human-wildlife conflict. Conservation efforts are underway to protect these magnificent creatures and their habitats.
