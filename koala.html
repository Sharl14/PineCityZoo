KOALA

Koalas are marsupials native to Australia, known for their distinctive appearance and tree-dwelling lifestyle. They have a unique diet consisting mainly of eucalyptus leaves, which are toxic to most animals.

Koalas are primarily nocturnal and spend most of their time sleeping in trees. They have a specialized digestive system that allows them to break down the tough eucalyptus leaves they consume. Koalas are also known for their vocalizations, which can be quite loud and distinctive.

Koalas are social animals and often live in small groups called colonies. They have a strong sense of smell and use scent marking to communicate with each other. Unfortunately, koalas face threats from habitat loss, climate change, and disease, making conservation efforts crucial for their survival.
